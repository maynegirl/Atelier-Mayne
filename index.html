The Practice

Rooted in tradition.
Refined for today.

"The finest spaces are not designed — they are remembered. Woven from the textures, rhythms, and stories of our heritage."

Atelier Mayne is a boutique interior design practice founded by Anjali Mayne — architect, interior designer, and student of Indian spatial tradition. Guided by the rich vocabulary of heritage craft — hand-carved lattice, inlaid stone, the geometry of classical Indian architecture, the warmth of vernacular homes — each commission is an exercise in considered restraint and deep cultural intention.

We work with a carefully selected few clients each year. Not by exclusion, but by conviction: that exceptional work demands singular focus.

Anjali Mayne
Founder & Principal Designer

The World We Draw From

Every space we conceive begins long before a brief — in the memory of a carved doorway, the weight of a handwoven textile, the silence of a courtyard at noon.

How We Work

The process

01

Consultation

We begin with unhurried conversation. Understanding your way of living, your sensibility, your relationship with material and memory — before a single line is drawn.

02

Concept & Research

A design concept rooted in place, culture, and client. We draw from Indian spatial philosophy — vastu, sacred geometry, craft lineage — and translate it into a vision that is wholly contemporary.

03

Design & Craft

Every surface, joint, and material is specified with care. We work closely with master karigars and traditional workshops across India, ensuring the design language lives in the making as much as the drawing.

04

The Living Space

A home returned to you — complete, considered, and alive with the craft and cultural depth that defines every Atelier Mayne commission. Not a handover. A beginning.
